# Executive Dashboard (Program-Level Visibility)

A concise, decision-oriented dashboard that provides executives with real-time insight into delivery health, risks, and outcomes across teams and programs.

---

## Purpose

To enable fast, informed decision-making by presenting a single, trusted view of program execution, predictability, and business impact.

---

## Intended Audience

- Executives and Senior Leaders  
- Portfolio / Program Leadership  
- Release Train Engineers (RTEs)  
- Enterprise Delivery Leads  

---

## Core Principles

- **Outcome over Output** – Focus on value delivered, not activity.
- **Leading Indicators** – Surface risks early, not after failure.
- **Actionable** – Every metric should prompt a decision or action.
- **Consistent** – Standard definitions across teams and programs.

---

## Dashboard Sections

### 1. Delivery Health (At-a-Glance)

| Metric | Description | Signal |
|------|-------------|--------|
| PI Predictability | Planned vs achieved objectives | Green / Amber / Red |
| Sprint Predictability | Commitment vs completion trend | Trend arrow |
| Flow Efficiency | Active work vs wait time | % |
| Throughput | Items completed per sprint | Trend |

---

### 2. Progress Against Commitments

- PI Objectives status (On Track / At Risk / Off Track)
- Sprint Goal achievement rate
- Feature / Capability burn-up
- Milestone confidence level

**Key Question:** Are we on track to deliver what we committed?

---

### 3. Risk & Dependency Overview

- Top 5 active risks with exposure rating
- Cross-team and external dependencies
- Aging risks and blocked dependencies
- Escalations requiring leadership action

**Key Question:** What could derail delivery if not addressed now?

---

### 4. Quality & Stability Signals

- Defect trends (new vs escaped)
- Rework rate
- Production incidents (if applicable)
- Test automation coverage trend

**Key Question:** Are we delivering sustainably?

---

### 5. Value & Outcomes

- Business outcomes achieved this PI
- Customer or stakeholder feedback highlights
- Adoption or usage signals (where available)
- Value delivered vs planned value

**Key Question:** Is delivery translating into business value?

---

### 6. Capacity & Focus

- Planned vs actual capacity usage
- Unplanned work percentage
- Context switching indicators
- Team stability signals

**Key Question:** Are teams focused on the right work?

---

## Standard RAG Definitions

- **Green:** On track, no action needed
- **Amber:** Risk emerging, mitigation in progress
- **Red:** Action required from leadership

---

## Executive Actions Enabled

- Reprioritize scope or funding
- Remove organizational impediments
- Resolve cross-program dependencies
- Adjust delivery strategy or timelines

---

## Governance Cadence

- **Weekly:** Delivery health and risk review
- **Sprint:** Progress and predictability update
- **PI:** Outcomes, value realization, and strategy adjustment

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Vanity metrics without decisions
- Overloading dashboards with team-level detail
- Lagging indicators only
- Inconsistent metric definitions

---

## Guiding Principle

> If a metric does not drive a decision or action, it does not belong on the executive dashboard.
